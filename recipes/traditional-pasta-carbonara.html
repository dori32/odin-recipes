Traditional Pasta Carbonara

Description

Searching for an authentic Italian recipe for pasta alla carbonara? Here's the original way to cook carbonara, with tips and tricks from Fine Dining Lovers.

Ingredients

  • 320g spaghetti pasta
  • 5 egg yolks
  • 150 g guanciale
  • 50 g grated pecorino romano
  • salt to taste
  • black pepper to taste

    • Steps

    1. To prepare the carbonara, start by heating the pasta water. While waiting for the water to boil, prepare the guanciale. Remove any rind and slice into strips, about 1 cm thick. Our tip: use less, rather than more water for the pasta. Cooking the pasta in less water will give you creamier results as the starch will be concentrated in less liquid.
    2. Heat a non-stick pan. When hot, add the guanciale strips.
    3. Cook the guanciale until it becomes crunchy, then remove it from the pan with a slotted spoon and place on a sheet of absorbent paper. Spread the guanciale out well, which should help keep its crunchiness.
    4. Leave the guanciale's cooking fat in the pan, it's vital for dressing the carbonara later to add flavour to the pasta.
    5. When the pasta water boils, add salt and then the spaghetti.
    6. While the spaghetti is cooking, break the eggs and place the yolks in a very large glass bowl.
    7. Beat the egg yolks and add the pepper and grated pecorino. Mix well with a whisk until you get a firm mixture.
    8. Drain the pasta al dente, keeping back a glass of its cooking water. Toss the spaghetti quickly in the guanciale's remaining cooking fat.
    9. Add a few tablespoons of the pasta cooking water to the egg mixture and dilute it to obtain a thick and enveloping cream.
    10. At this point, put the spaghetti in a bowl. Add the egg mixture and mix quickly to amalgamate all the ingredients.
    11. Add the crispy guanciale, stir one last time and serve your original spaghetti carbonara immediately.
